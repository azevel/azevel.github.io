How Did Russian GDP Grow in 2025?

March 30, 2026

I looked at the 1% GDP growth figure for Russia in 2025. Since mid-2022, Rosstat has limited or classified certain economic datasets to prevent "sanctions-related vulnerabilities." As a result, a detailed breakdown of the GDP is harder to verify. However, the Industrial Production Index, which measures the output of every factory, mine, and power plant, reveals the core trends.

Total manufacturing for the full year 2025 grew by 3.6%. In December 2025 alone, manufacturing output jumped 7.8% compared to the previous December. This growth is concentrated in specific sectors: Other Transport Equipment (+32.0% for aircraft, tanks, and drones), Finished Metal Products (+18.0% for artillery shells and armor plating), and Computers, Electronics, and Optics (+11.7% for guidance systems and semiconductors).

The most telling data point is the Finished Metal Products category in December 2025. It saw a surge of 156.8% compared to November. While these three sectors skyrocketed, the rest of the industrial base struggled. Mining, coal, water supply, and civilian manufacturing all declined. The government is the primary buyer, printing rubles to fund equipment like tanks, drones, and navigation systems that are immediately sent to the front and destroyed. This adds to GDP but subtracts from national wealth.
