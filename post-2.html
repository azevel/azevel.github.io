The Second Entry

March 30, 2026

I was told that to be a writer, one must first learn to sit in a room alone until the walls begin to speak. I have been in this room for three days. The walls haven't said a word, but the radiator is making a rhythmic clicking sound that I am reasonably certain is Morse code for "get a real job."

They say Hemingway wrote standing up. I tried that once, but my legs fell asleep and I fell into my bookshelf, knocking over a first edition copy of *Ulysses* that I haven't actually read. Is this the craft? Is this the "tortured soul" aesthetic everyone on the internet seems to be selling? I have four empty coffee cups on my desk and a draft that consists entirely of the word "nevermore" repeated until the font became too small to see.

If anyone finds this, please send more ink. And perhaps a sandwich. The pursuit of the perfect footnote is a lonely and hungry road.
